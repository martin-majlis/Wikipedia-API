Crust
Earth's crust may be divided into oceanic and continental crust. The oceanic crust is predominantly basaltic, while the continental crust has more of lower density materials such as granite, metamorphic rocks and sediments, which while covering nearly 75% of the continental surfaces make only about 5% of the mass of the crust.
While the oceanic crust is covered by the ocean, the ocean also covers continental crust, the continental shelves, which makes these parts of the ocean shelf seas. The oceanic crust forms large oceanic basins with features like abyssal plains, seamounts, submarine volcanoes, oceanic trenches, submarine canyons, oceanic plateaus, and a globe-spanning mid-ocean ridge system. This submarine terrain of the ocean floor has an average bathymetric depth of 4 km, and is as varied as the terrain above sea level.
